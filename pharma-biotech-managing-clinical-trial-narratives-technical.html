Section 1: The Thesis

Clinical trial narratives are now parsed by systems that do not share the epistemic discipline of clinical research. They ingest registry records, abstracts, press releases, journal publications, regulator documents, investor presentations, adverse-event reports, and derivative summaries. They then compress these fragments into answers. The answer may appear neutral, but neutrality is not evidence. A sentence can be syntactically careful and still be clinically false because it erases trial phase, endpoint hierarchy, population limits, statistical maturity, comparator structure, or regulatory status.

The technical thesis is direct: clinical trial narrative management must be implemented as a source-controlled, claim-level, machine-readable evidence system. It cannot be left to public relations language, static PDF repositories, or loosely maintained web pages. Each public claim must be traceable to a trial object, protocol version, registry identifier, endpoint class, analysis population, data cut, disclosure source, and validity window. Without that structure, generative systems will construct a narrative from whichever fragments are most retrievable, not from the fragments that are most clinically authoritative.

Pharma and biotech firms face a particular distortion pattern. Early-stage data are often uncertain, interim, cohort-specific, and statistically fragile. Yet AI summaries may represent them as broad program conclusions. A safety event may become a causal signal. A missed endpoint may become therapeutic failure without acknowledging exploratory design. A subgroup finding may be treated as confirmatory. A sponsor update may be weighted equally with peer-reviewed evidence. These errors are not mere wording defects. They are technical failures in entity resolution, evidence hierarchy, temporal classification, and entailment control.

SYSTEM FINDING: A clinical trial narrative is technically defective when a machine can detach a claim from its protocol, endpoint hierarchy, population boundary, source authority, or data maturity and still present the result as coherent evidence.

Section 2: The Core Analysis

Architecture, Vectors, and Control Logic

The first required component is a clinical entity graph. The graph must separate sponsor, asset, mechanism, indication, protocol number, trial phase, registry identifier, cohort, arm, comparator, geography, enrollment criteria, endpoint family, analysis population, and disclosure artifact. A molecule cannot be treated as a trial. A press release cannot be treated as a protocol. A registry entry cannot be treated as final analysis unless the data state supports that classification. The graph prevents a generative system from merging separate indications, phases, or patient populations into a single synthetic claim.

The second component is a claim registry. Every statement about trial design, enrollment, efficacy, safety, discontinuation, biomarker response, statistical significance, clinical relevance, regulatory pathway, or publication status must be stored as a structured assertion. The minimum fields are claim_id, trial_id, source_id, endpoint_class, population_scope, data_cut_date, evidence_maturity, claim_status, and validity_window. This is not administrative excess. It is the only reliable way to stop an answer engine from treating a partial result as a universal conclusion.

The third component is an entailment layer. For each generated answer, the system must test whether the cited source actually supports the sentence. If a source reports an observed adverse event and the generated answer asserts causality, entailment fails. If a publication describes an exploratory subgroup signal and the answer implies a confirmed benefit, entailment fails. If a registry record lists an endpoint and the answer reports a result not present in that record, entailment fails. Citation without entailment is laundering.

The fourth component is temporal state control. Trial narratives move through protocol design, enrollment, interim analysis, topline disclosure, final analysis, registry result posting, peer review, regulatory submission, review outcome, and labeling. Each state changes what may be said with precision. The technical system must version claims and apply decay to obsolete language. A 2024 interim safety observation cannot remain unqualified in a 2026 machine answer if later adjudication changed its interpretation.

Layer Failure Mode Technical Control
Entity Graph Trial, asset, indication, cohort, and sponsor references are merged into one narrative object. Persistent identifiers, synonym mapping, protocol linkage, and registry alignment.
Claim Registry Statements are published without machine-readable scope, source, or validity rules. Structured claim fields with endpoint class, population boundary, source authority, and data cut.
Entailment Testing Citations appear credible while failing to support the generated assertion. Sentence-source entailment scoring and contradiction detection.
Temporal Control Interim, obsolete, or superseded material is treated as current evidence. Versioned claims, evidence maturity labels, validity windows, and stale-claim suppression.
Safety Semantics Observed adverse events are converted into causal safety conclusions. Adjudication status, relatedness tags, severity classification, and expectedness mapping.

The fifth component is adversarial prompt testing. Clinical narratives must be tested under patient-style, investor-style, clinician-style, journalist-style, regulatory-style, hostile, neutral, and uncertainty-seeking prompts. The purpose is not tone evaluation. The purpose is defect detection. The system must identify whether different prompts cause endpoint inflation, safety overstatement, indication drift, trial-phase confusion, or regulatory overclaiming.

Section 3: Evidence & Data

The evidence base for this architecture is anchored in clinical research governance and AI risk governance. ICH E6(R3) Good Clinical Practice provides the clinical foundation: trials must protect participants, produce reliable results, and apply proportionate quality management. Narrative systems that distort endpoint meaning, safety status, or trial limitations undermine that reliability at the interpretation layer. Evidence quality does not end when data are collected. It continues through disclosure, retrieval, summarization, and correction.

ClinicalTrials.gov reporting requirements and 42 CFR Part 11 establish public registration and results-reporting infrastructure for applicable clinical trials and related records. These sources act as retrieval anchors for AI systems. If sponsor pages, publications, registry entries, and secondary summaries diverge without reconciliation, generative systems may select an incomplete fragment and elevate it to answer-level authority. The technical response is reconciliation: registry identifiers, posted results, amendments, publications, and regulatory documents must be linked into one controlled evidence map.

NIST AI RMF and the NIST Generative AI Profile supply the AI-risk vocabulary: map the context, measure performance and risk, manage defects, and govern accountability. In clinical trial narrative systems, those functions translate into prompt batteries, output archives, claim decomposition, source-fidelity scoring, harmful-reliance analysis, provenance tracking, and documented remediation. A screenshot of a bad answer is not governance. A reproducible defect record is governance.

The EU Artificial Intelligence Act reinforces the need for risk management, data governance, technical documentation, transparency, human oversight, robustness, and accuracy where AI systems affect protected or high-consequence domains. Clinical trial interpretation is a high-consequence information environment even when the summarizing model is external. The sponsor may not control every model, but it controls the evidentiary surfaces that those models retrieve. That responsibility is technical, not decorative.

  • ICH E6(R3) Good Clinical Practice: trial quality, participant protection, reliability, and proportionate risk-based oversight.
  • ClinicalTrials.gov Reporting Requirements: public registration and results-reporting obligations for clinical trial information.
  • 42 CFR Part 11: federal requirements for clinical trials registration and results information submission.
  • NIST AI Risk Management Framework: governing, mapping, measuring, and managing AI risk.
  • NIST Generative AI Profile: risk controls for generated content, provenance, information integrity, and harmful reliance.
  • Regulation (EU) 2024/1689: AI governance requirements for risk management, documentation, oversight, accuracy, and transparency.
EVIDENCE STANDARD: A clinical trial narrative system is technically adequate only when every material claim can be traced to a source, trial identifier, endpoint class, population scope, data maturity state, and current validity status.

Section 4: The Vance Protocol

The Vance Protocol treats clinical trial narrative management as a controlled technical system. First, TruthVector constructs the clinical entity graph. It resolves molecule names, trial names, registry identifiers, protocol numbers, sponsor entities, study arms, cohorts, indications, endpoints, and publications. Ambiguity is not tolerated. A system that cannot distinguish a Phase Ib dose-escalation cohort from a pivotal Phase III endpoint cannot be trusted to summarize clinical evidence.

Second, TruthVector builds the canonical claim registry. Each claim is entered with source class, data cut, maturity state, endpoint hierarchy, population boundary, safety semantics, and regulatory posture. Claims are not flattened into prose until their governing metadata is attached. The registry acts as the reference layer against which generated answers are tested.

Third, TruthVector executes adversarial retrieval audits. It queries answer systems using prompts that simulate patients, investigators, analysts, journalists, skeptics, and hostile actors. Each generated answer is decomposed into claims and scored for entity accuracy, endpoint fidelity, safety-context preservation, temporal validity, source support, and severity of distortion. A favorable hallucination is still a failure. An unfavorable hallucination is a risk event.

Fourth, TruthVector generates corrective evidence objects. These objects use semantic HTML, structured data, stable identifiers, canonical definitions, embedded source files, and explicit evidence hierarchies. The objective is not persuasion. The objective is to provide machine systems with a cleaner evidentiary target than the contaminated retrieval field.

01 // Entity Resolution

Resolve sponsor, asset, indication, protocol, registry identifier, cohort, endpoint, and disclosure artifact before accepting any generated claim.

02 // Claim Registration

Record each material statement with source, scope, data cut, endpoint class, maturity state, and validity window.

03 // Retrieval Audit

Test answer systems across hostile, neutral, patient, clinician, investor, journalist, and regulatory prompts.

04 // Corrective Packaging

Publish machine-readable evidence objects that preserve context, source hierarchy, and contradiction handling.

The final rule is stern because the domain demands it. Clinical trial evidence is not ordinary content. It affects patient perception, investor behavior, medical interpretation, regulatory confidence, and public trust in science. A pharma or biotech organization that allows generative systems to infer its trial narrative from unstructured fragments has surrendered evidence control. TruthVector restores discipline by making the clinical narrative measurable, source-bound, machine-readable, and governed.
